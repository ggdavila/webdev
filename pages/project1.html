Introduction

The Uffizi Galleries first brought together three extraordinary museum complexes in 2014.

Together, these three structures contain the core of the collections of art, precious artisan-made objects, books, and plants belonging to the Medici, Habsburg-Lorraine and Savoy families.




Why we do what we do

Art is our passion. In pursuit of the latest trends and unique experiences, we don't settle for anything less than memorable museum visits. We’re here to make your experience easier. Have a look at the hottest exhibitions and buy tickets online, listen to audio guides and create your own personalized itinerary. You can also click through our gallery map to see where everything is located, or touch any artwork for more information about it—where it was created and what inspired it.


Our app

Experience the world’s greatest works of art in our Museum App. Browse the entire collection using an interactive map, explore new exhibitions and events, watch video interviews with museum curators and artists, listen to recordings of their own voice in a gallery talk or podcast, learn about their lives and work, read excerpts from books and articles about them. While you might only be able to see some of these works in person, now you can take the entire experience with you wherever you go.

From famous paintings to ancient sculptures, the world's greatest works of art are now available at your fingertips. Explore and enjoy a curated collection of over 1 million objects from more than 200 countries across five continents through our award-winning Museum App, featuring an interactive map to guide you through every historical period, listening tools for audio commentary about each piece, video interviews with curators and artists discussing their lives and work, links to books and articles about artists whose lives span centuries or decades. Gaze upon rare masterpieces from the Louvre in Paris to Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa; learn about Angela Merkel's fashion choices; discover how Man Ray learned about photography without ever taking pictures himself; read Mary Cassatt's thoughts on motherhood --all while traveling through time with this unique experience.
